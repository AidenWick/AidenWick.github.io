What is does an Accessible Website Have?

Universal Design

Univeral design is an approach to design that has the goal of creating an environment that is accessible by of all ages, abilities, and backgrounds. There shouldn't be a need for adaptation or specialized design.

The Seven Key Principles

Equitable Use:

This is to make the design useful and marketable to people with diverse abilities.

Flexibility in Use:

The design accomidates a wide range of of preferences and abilities.

Simple and Intuitive Use:

The use of the design is very easy to understand.

Perceptible Information:

The design communicates the information effectively to the user. This can be through visual, auditory, or tactile modes.

Tolerance for Error:

The design minimizes the consiquences of accidental mistakes, such as misclicks. You can easily reverse any actions or correct errors.

Low Physical Effort:

The design can be used efficently and comfortably with minimum effort. Basically so it doesnt require a ton of excessive force.

Size and Space for Approach and Use:

Appropriate size and space are used, regardless of how the user's size, posture, or mobility.
